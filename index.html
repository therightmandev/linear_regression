How the function is obtained

The function is \(y = mx + b\) where $$m = r{S_y \over S_x}$$ $$r = {\sum ((x-\bar x)(y-\bar y)) \over \sqrt{\sum (x-\bar x)^2 \sum (y-\bar y)^2}}$$ $$ S_y = \sum (y-\bar y)^2$$ $$S_x = \sum (x-\bar x)^2$$ \(r\) is the correlation coefficient, \(S_y\) and \(S_x\) are the standard deviations of x and y.
To calculate \(b\), we just need the slope and the averages $$ b = \bar y - m\bar x $$
